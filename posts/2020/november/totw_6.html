Tunes of the Week #3

Some Rap and R&b elements from Otik and Nikki Nair..

Posted on 30/11/2020

Weekly Updated Spotify Playlist

Each week, I, along with two friends, will add 5 songs each to a spotify playlist. Check out the Plant Bass's Picks Playlist and give it a follow for up to date tunes.
Here, I will focus on my selection of tracks.

Salute – Kudi

130 BPM Electronica.
“I’ve been making a lot more dancey stuff recently… kudi is hausa for 'money' ”. Dancey it is, this track has a groovy bassline and bouncy open hats simply made for the clubs. Buy it on Beatport.

Nikki Nair – Power Tool

137 BPM breaks.
Nikki Nair samples “Packed up my tools for my raw power move” from The Notorious BIG - Everyday Struggle in this surprising release from the Dirtybirds label. The usual tech house record label by Claude VonStroke branches out into different genres and brings a really unique Drum & Bass track. The liquid bass and banging snare stand out the most. The artworks on this label are fantastic too. Buy it on Bandcamp.

Ruff Stuff – Would You Like To See

130 BPM Deep House
A Deep electro collab between Italian man, Ruff Stuff, and Berlin resident, Black Loops. Dreamy vocals from a woman wave in and out of this tune. Buy it on Bandcamp.

Otik – Seasonal FX

140 BPM Breaks
Instantly recognized vocal chop from Swae Lee’s line in Travis Scott – Sicko Mode. The lyric is so recognised he had the inspiration to create a new song based around it. Hard breaks balanced with soft harmonising pads. Buy it on Bandcamp.

The Blaze – Territory

120 BPM Electronica
One of the best live acts I have seen. The Blaze are full of emotion and their music leaves you with shivers. The French duo build their tracks on feelings and invite you to embrace in the sentiment of the song. Their music videos complement their songs too by spreading that message of love. Buy it on Amazon.
