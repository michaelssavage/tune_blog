Where do beginner DJs get their songs?

A look at options for downloading new music.

Posted on 21/11/2020

The expected answer for downloading music files is buying it on any number of legal music websites. Bandcamp, Beatport, and iTunes offer good quality music downloads. Even using music pools and paying a fee every month to get access to certain genres is recommended for DJs.

For beginner DJs though, who are cash-strapped or aren’t sure if they can devote hours to this hobby, it is not an option. Resorting to Youtube rips just can’t cut it. The quality of the sound would be humiliating to play out live. So what are the alternatives? This is an educative look at the different applications around.

SoulSeek

This is an excellent resource. It is a file-sharing client made in 2006 that encourages sharing organized files with each other and you can find some very rare music. I’ve personally found Disco & Soul music from the 80s in high quality bitrates.

If you’re looking for some underground or relatively unknown music, this is a good place to start. Just because it is an old, old program, don’t be put off.

Deezer

Deezer is a competitor to Spotify with one difference being people have learned to extract the music files from the code. This isn’t possible in Spotify because the files are well encrypted. There have been applications floating around the likes of Github and other repositories that have fully functional downloaders of these songs. Certainly not legal but very interesting to read about. You can find out more info on Reddit.com/r/deemix.

“deemix is a python library that lets you download millions of songs, soundtracks, albums in high-quality mp3 and FLAC. Here you can find discussions about the apps that use the deemix library. Deemix is meant to replace Deezloader Remix.”
