Tunes of the Week #3

Some Rap and R&b elements from Otik and Nikki Nair..

Posted on 30/11/2020

Weekly Updated Spotify Playlist

Each week, I, along with two friends, will add 5 songs each to a spotify playlist. Check out the Plant Bass's Picks Playlist and give it a follow for up to date tunes.
Here, I will focus on my selection of tracks.

Salute – Kudi

130 BPM Electronica
Buy it on Beatport.
“I’ve been making a lot more dancey stuff recently… kudi is hausa for 'money' ”. Dancey it is, this track has a groovy bassline and bouncy open hats simply made for the clubs.

Nikki Nair – Power Tool

137 BPM breaks
Buy it on Bandcamp.
Samples The Notorious BIG - Everyday Struggle, “Packed up my tools for my raw power move”. A surprising release from the Dirtybirds label. The usual tech house record label by Claude VonStroke branches out into different genres and brings a really unique Drum & Bass track. The liquid bass and banging snare stand out the most. The artworks on this label are fantastic too.

Ruff Stuff – Would You Like To See

130 BPM Deep House
Buy it on Bandcamp.
A Deep electro collab between Italian man, Ruff Stuff, and Berlin resident, Black Loops. Dreamy vocals from a woman wave in and out of this tune.

Otik – Seasonal FX

140 BPM Breaks
Buy it on Bandcamp.
Instantly recognized vocal chop from Swae Lee’s line in Travis Scott – Sicko Mode. The lyric is so recognised he had the inspiration to create a new song based around. Hard breaks balanced with soft harmonising pads.

The Blaze – Territory

120 BPM Electronica
Buy it on Amazon.
One of the best live acts you can see. The Blaze are full of emotion and their music leaves you with shivers. The French duo build their tracks on feelings and invite you to embrace in the sentiment of the song. Their music videos complement their songs too by spreading the message that the song tries to translate.
